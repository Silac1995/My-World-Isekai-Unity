Part 1: Facing Bug Fix

Principle: Only YOU control YOUR facing
A character's facing direction is determined solely by their own chosen target. When ATK 2 calls SetPlannedTarget(defender), it changes ATK 2's facing — never the defender's. The defender faces whoever he chose to target, and only his own AI can change that.

Part 4: Character Behaviors by State

⏱ Waiting (initiative charging)
• Idle sway: subtle random drift (~0.5m radius) around current position
• Tactical circling: if your side outnumbers 2:1+, melee characters slowly orbit the outnumbered enemies
• Stay within soft anchor leash radius
⚔️ Attacking (turn active)
• Melee: advance into strike range, attack, then back off slightly to create a gap (dynamic spacing, no fixed "home")
• Ranged: shoot from current position, no approach needed
• Both sides walk toward each other to close initial distance — melee goes further, ranged stops at comfort range
🧍 Unengaged (no mutual target)
• Barely moves — stays near starting position
• Faces their chosen target (even if not mutual yet)
• Waits for target to reciprocate, forming the engagement
💀 Target Died
• Immediately auto-acquire new target
• Join or form engagement based on new target's status
• Begin closing distance toward new engagement

Part 5: Ranged Character Rules

Ranged keeps distance, does NOT flee
• Ranged characters position further from opponents (back of engagement)
• When their target approaches them to attack, they do NOT run away
• They hold their ground and take the hit — this ensures ranged characters can actually be killed
• They only reposition to maintain range after they finish their own attack turn, not reactively

Part 6: What Gets Reworked

🛑 Replace / Heavy Rework
• CombatEngagementCoordinator — new relationship-based grouping algorithm
• CombatFormation — replace fixed slot rings with dynamic organic positioning
• CombatTacticalPacer — rework for dynamic spacing, idle sway, circling behavior
• CharacterVisual facing — single source of truth (own target only)
✅ Keep / Light Touch
• BattleManager — tick system, initiative, team management stays
• CombatEngagement — two-group structure stays, internals change
• EngagementGroup — member tracking stays, center calculation stays
• CharacterCombat — action/intent system stays, SetPlannedTarget decoupled from facing
